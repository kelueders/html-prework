My Journey Into Coding

Life has been a winding road

My interest in computers began as a teenager as I attempted to build websites in order to collaborate with others on roleplaying websites about Harry Potter and other fantasy worlds. Because of this interest I took several programming classes in high school, but had an uninspiring teacher so I veered toward the physical sciences in college. I majored in Biology with a plan to go into some area of research. After working through several internships I realized a PhD in a specific sector of the biological sciences was not of interest to me. At that time I considered returning to school to become a computer programmer, but the path seemed too long given my background in biology and I had never heard of bootcamps. So instead I become a physical therapist. I was interested in health and fitness, and figured I could help people and solve problems in that field. All the while I wondered what could have been had I gone into software. And unforunately after six years in the healthcare field, I have burned out due to the rigors of patient care. I became inspired, though, while I was working for an up-and-coming physical therapy company based out of California that had designed an app through which all documentation, communication, records, home exercises, and staff assistance could be provided. The app made my job so much easier. And the company now is rapidly growing. I realized there is a lot of potential in the healthcare industry for software like this that could help healthcare workers, lessening burnout and improving outcomes. I do feel the industry could use software developers with a clinical background in order to fully optimize the systems and advance healthcare in general into this new technological era.
